# IRB / Ethics Note for Annotation

This document is not legal advice and does not replace institutional review.

The annotation activity uses synthetic case files and asks annotators to judge AI-agent outputs. It does not ask annotators to disclose personal experiences, sensitive demographic data, medical records, or private information. However, because the resulting annotations may be analyzed for publication, the project team should ask the relevant IRB, ethics board, or research-compliance office for a determination before treating annotation data as publishable human-subjects data.

## Suggested determination request

Ask the institution whether the activity is:

1. not human-subjects research;
2. exempt human-subjects research;
3. expedited/minimal-risk human-subjects research; or
4. requires another form of review.

## Information to provide

- Cases are synthetic.
- Annotators judge AI-agent outputs, not their own experiences.
- No sensitive personal data or demographic data is collected unless the IRB approves it.
- Annotator names are replaced by IDs in analysis files.
- Results are reported in aggregate.
- Participation is voluntary.
- Students or employees are not penalized for declining.
- If paid, annotators receive reasonable compensation independent of label content.
- The private condition mapping is hidden from annotators until labels are frozen.

## Extra caution with students and employees

If annotators are students, employees, or people under the supervision of an author, add safeguards against perceived pressure. Do not tie participation to grades or employment evaluation unless an approved protocol and comparable alternatives exist.
